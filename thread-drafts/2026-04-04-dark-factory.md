# Thread

**1/**
The dark factory isn't coming. It's already being built — by non-coders.

**2/**
GitHub: 1B commits in 2025. Now 275M/week — Kyle Daigle says that's pace for 14B by year end.
Most people read this as "AI writes more code."
Wrong frame.
The real signal: the person giving the orders isn't an engineer anymore.

**3/**
I'm not a programmer.
Over the past month I vibe-coded the entire @AgenticNow Telegram pipeline — automated ingestion, curation, publishing.
Week one, API costs exploded. I didn't debug. I just cut Opus and moved down a tier.
That's not coding. That's resource allocation. Commander decisions.

**4/**
This is what "dark factory" means at human scale.
Not robots on a floor you never see.
It's you — running operations you were never supposed to be qualified for.
The bottleneck shifted from skill to clarity of intent.

**5/**
In the Agentic Web, the scarce resource isn't code. It isn't even AI access.
It's people who know what they want — precisely enough to direct a system that never sleeps.
Are you sharpening that muscle? Or are you still waiting for someone to tell you what to want?
